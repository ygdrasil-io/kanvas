## Bounded Facts

The supported AA clip scene is intentionally narrow:

- source: `AaclipGM` bounded AA clip rect grid;
- GPU route: `webgpu.coverage.aaclip-bounded-grid`;
- coverage strategy: `webgpu.coverage.aa-clip-rect-grid`;
- clip op: `intersect`;
- clip shape: `aa-rect-grid`;
- edge count: `90 / 256`;
- path contours: `15`;
- inverse clip: `false`;
- complex clip: `false`;
- dash pattern: `none`;
- GPU similarity: `98.83` at threshold `98.78`;
- fallback: `none`.

The route key remains:

```text
pathAA=aaclip clip=aaRectGrid op=intersect budget=current source=AaclipGM
```

## Boundary Policy

No arbitrary clip-stack support claim is added.

No threshold is lowered.

No integer scissor substitution is introduced.

The current nested rrect clip row is kept visible as a refusal:

```text
coverage.nested-clip-visual-parity-below-threshold
```

That boundary is under the numerical clip/edge budgets (`clipDepth=3/4`,
`edgeCount=72/256`) but still cannot be promoted because the WebGPU row remains
`expected-unsupported`.

Arbitrary AA clips and unsupported clip stacks keep the policy diagnostics:

```text
coverage.arbitrary-aa-clip-unsupported
geometry.clip-stack-unsupported
```

## Non-Claims

KAN-004 does not claim broad AA clip support.

KAN-004 does not claim arbitrary clip-stack support.

KAN-004 does not claim nested rrect clip WebGPU support.

KAN-004 does not support complex clips, inverse clips, shader clips, or
perspective clips.

KAN-004 does not replace arbitrary AA clips with integer scissor.

KAN-004 does not port Ganesh, Graphite, a SkSL compiler, SkSL IR, or a SkSL VM.
